Example 1

The chart represents the log of letters received by Lulu, including the number of letters received over time, the median age of the letters, the distribution of sending countries, and a table summarizing the letter counts by country.


Source: r/dataisbeautiful


Misleading elements:

  1. Dual-axis chart confusion: The bar chart showing the number of letters received and the median age of letters uses two different scales, which can make it difficult to compare trends accurately.

  2. Lack of color differentiation: All elements in the visualization use the same dark green color, making it difficult to distinguish between different data points, charts, and sections.

  3. Map lacks numerical clarity: The map shows countries that sent letters, but it does not provide numerical values, making it hard to interpret the actual volume of letters sent from each country.

  4. Lack of context: While the dashboard presents numbers effectively, it does not provide enough explanation on why the data matters or how it should be interpreted.

Data issues:

  1. Skewed dataset: The dataset is heavily dominated by the United States (473 out of 511 letters), but the visualization does not highlight this imbalance.

  2. Limited breakdown of letter age: The median age of letters is shown but lacks further breakdowns (e.g., by country or time period), reducing its usefulness.

Suggestion:

  1. Improve the dual-axis chart: Consider separating the letter count and median age into two different charts to reduce confusion. Alternatively, use a clear visual distinction (e.g., bars for count, dots/line for age).

  2. Introduce color differentiation: Use varied shades or different colors for different elements to improve readability and clarity.

  3. Enhance the map's functionality: Add numerical labels or a gradient scale to visually indicate letter volumes by country.

  4. Provide additional context: Explain the significance of the trends, such as why the letter volume fluctuates over time or what influences the letter age.

Example 2

The chart represents population change in the United States from 2020 to 2023 at the county level, showing areas of growth and decline. The color gradient represents percentage changes, with green indicating population growth and purple indicating population decline. Additionally, the chart highlights the fastest-growing and fastest-shrinking counties with a minimum population of 50,000.


Source: r/dataisbeautiful


Misleading elements:

  1. Uneven Classification Bins: The legend categories are inconsistent, with some having wider ranges, potentially exaggerating population decline.

  2. Overlapping Small Counties: Small counties blend together, making it hard to distinguish individual changes, especially in densely populated areas.

  3. Lack of Absolute Population Numbers: The map only shows percentage change, which may mislead viewers about the actual number of people moving in or out.

Data issues:

  1. No Distinction Between Migration and Natural Change: It is unclear whether population shifts are due to migration, birth rates, or death rates.

  2. Small Counties May Show Large Percentage Changes: A county with a small population might experience a dramatic percentage shift even if the actual number of people changing is small.

Suggestion:

  1. Standardize Bin Ranges: Use equal percentage intervals (e.g., every 5%) to prevent visual bias.

  2. Indicate Absolute Population Impact: Include a secondary visualization (e.g., a bubble chart) to show the actual number of people affected.
